## Applications of Multivariable Calculus

### 1. Physics Simulation & Computer Graphics

Pixar's animation rendering uses partial differential equations to simulate light scattering on surfaces. Water ripples, cloth motion, smoke dispersion — all numerical solutions of multivariable calculus.

### 2. Weather Maps

Isotherms (temperature contour lines) visualize the bivariate function T(x, y). Weather fronts follow the gradient's direction, and rainfall amounts are integrals of moisture flux divergence.

### 3. Marginal Rate of Substitution in Economics

A utility function U(x, y) measures satisfaction from consuming x units of good A and y units of good B. The partial derivative ∂U/∂x is the marginal utility of A. The marginal rate of substitution = (∂U/∂x)/(∂U/∂y) determines how consumers allocate their budget.

### 4. Electromagnetism

Maxwell's equations use divergence and curl to describe how electric and magnetic fields vary in space. These partial differential equations predicted the existence of electromagnetic waves — which is light.

### 5. Finite Element Analysis

Bridges, aircraft, and skyscrapers are structurally analyzed by cutting the object into thousands of tiny tetrahedral elements. Partial differential equations are solved on each element and assembled into the whole — all rooted in multivariable calculus.

### 6. Optimal Transport

How do you move a pile of sand to a target shape at minimum cost? The Monge-Kantorovich optimal transport problem solves PDEs and is applied to image registration, resource allocation, and domain adaptation in machine learning.

### 7. Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) & Recommendation Systems

Netflix's recommendation engine decomposes the user-movie rating matrix into three matrix factors. This uses Lagrange multipliers from multivariable calculus to find the optimal low-rank approximation.
