## Beauty in Integrals

### The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

$$\frac{d}{dx}\int_a^x f(t)\,dt = f(x)$$

$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a) \text{ where } F'(x) = f(x)$$

Differentiation and integration are inverse operations. One describes the rate of change, the other describes accumulation — and they're exact inverses. This may be the greatest discovery in mathematical history.

### The Gaussian Integral

$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2}\,dx = \sqrt{\pi}$$

This integral gives the normalizing constant of the normal distribution. Why does e^{-x²} from -∞ to ∞ equal √π? e and π meet again, unexpectedly.

### The Gamma Function — Factorials Generalized

$$\Gamma(n) = \int_0^\infty x^{n-1}e^{-x}\,dx$$

When n is a positive integer, Γ(n) = (n-1)!. This integral extends the factorial to all real numbers and even complex numbers. Γ(1/2) = √π — π appears once more.

### Wallis Product

$$\frac{\pi}{2} = \frac{2\cdot2}{1\cdot3} \cdot \frac{4\cdot4}{3\cdot5} \cdot \frac{6\cdot6}{5\cdot7} \cdots$$

An infinite product equals π/2. Derived through integration — definite integrals revealing deep truths about π.

### Elliptic Integrals

Computing an ellipse's arc length invariably leads to $\int \sqrt{1-k^2\sin^2\theta}\,d\theta$. This integral has no elementary antiderivative — it defines entirely new functions (elliptic functions), opening doors to deeper mathematics.
